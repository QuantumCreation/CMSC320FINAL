INTRODUCTION

As the world entered the 21st century, technology and online media has slowly come to dominate our lives. Every day we are bombarded by news of wars, terrorism, domestic inequality, corruption, homocides, and political instability. However, many others, such as Steven Pinker have proposed that despite the bleak image that the media has painted, the world at large has in fact been improving over the last few centuries, and most drastically in the last few decades. This makes us ask, which view is correct? Has the world improved, despite the many horrors we regularly hear of in the news? Most of has have heard of the military buildup in China, the Russian takeover of Crimea and of the domestic political violence in the US. Despite these events and the many other problems we hear of so often, have the lives of the average human on Earth improved? Or have certain countries improved as others are left in the dust? Or perhaps, is the news correct and the world is headed in a downward spiral? Using a number of data sets from the US government and 3rd party organizations, I will attempt to shed some light on whether or not the world at large has improved for the average citizen with various measures from economic stability and freedom to happiness, poverty, homocides and other statistical measures.

In the next few lines, you will see I load the different data sets. I have renamed the csv files so that it is clear what they are. Each of these data sets include a number of countries from around the world across the last few decades.

The Development Index is an index that uses life expectancy, access to education, and standard of living to generate an index value.

The Homocide data is the number of homocides per 100,000 individuals in the country. It is a rate, not a number, hence it scales with the size of the country in question.

The Hunger Index tells uses required caloric needs and nutritional needs to generate a value. For example, an individual could have more than enough calories to survive, but lack necessary vitamins and minerals leading to sickness, disease, or even death in extreme cases.

The Cantril Happiness data is a scale where respondents are asked to rate their lives on a scale from 1-10, where 10 is the happiest, and 1 is the most depressed.

The Equality data is the percent difference between the wages of men and women in various countries over time.

The Economy data has many different measures, but what I will be looking at is the economic freedom summary index, a measure of how free individuals are in a country to pursue economic oppurtunities.
